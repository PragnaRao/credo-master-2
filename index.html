Coreographer | Artist

Choreography is the art and practice of designing sequences of movements and steps to be performed by dancers or performers. It is a creative process that involves the arrangement of movements in time and space to create a visually compelling and expressive performance. Choreography can be applied to various forms of dance, theater, film, television, and other performance arts.

Services

Wedding

Choreography at weddings has become increasingly popular, adding a touch of creativity and entertainment to the celebration. Couples often choose to include choreographed dance routines in various parts of their wedding festivities.

Events

Choreography at events can enhance the overall experience, engage the audience, and add an entertaining and artistic element to various occasions. Whether it's a corporate event, product launch, conference, or a social gathering, choreographed performances can leave a lasting impression.

Conferences

Choreography at conferences can be a creative and impactful way to engage attendees, break up the traditional format, and communicate key messages in a memorable manner. Incorporating dance or movement into conferences can add an element of entertainment, energize the audience, and create a dynamic atmosphere.

Culture

Choreography plays a significant role in cultural events, helping to express traditions, celebrate heritage, and showcase the diversity of different communities. Cultural events often feature dance and movement as a central component, providing a platform for artistic expression and storytelling.

About Me

Pragna Rao MN ,I was born on March 25, 2005, in Bangalore, Karnataka. From a young age, I exhibited an innate passion for movement and dance, immersing myself in various dance forms that would eventually shape my distinctive choreographic style.

I'm also committed to dance education and mentorship. I'm waiting forward to frequently conduct masterclasses and workshops, inspiring emerging dancers to embrace creativity, individuality, and the transformative power of movement.
